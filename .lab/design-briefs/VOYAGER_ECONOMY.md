## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Go-To-Market:** B2B2C - Target community builders, migrate their communities to Voyager.

## INTELLIGENT MODEL ROUTING

### How It Works

**Automatic Classification:**
Every Navigator query is analyzed for complexity before routing to the appropriate model.

**Simple Queries â†’ Fast Model (Haiku):**
- Definitions ("What's a STAR story?")
- Quick facts ("How long should my resume be?")
- Single-step answers ("What does this term mean?")
- **Response time:** <1 second

**Complex Queries â†’ Reasoning Model (Llama/Sonnet):**
- Multi-step reasoning ("Help me choose between two career paths")
- Strategic thinking ("How should I approach this negotiation?")
- Emotional support ("I'm struggling with imposter syndrome")
- Career planning ("I want to pivot but don't know where to start")
- **Response time:** 2-4 seconds

**Classification Method:**
- Rule-based heuristics (message length, keywords, context depth)
- Falls back to Haiku pre-classification for uncertain cases
- Optimized for accuracy ~90%+
- Defaults to better model when uncertain

### Usage Scenarios

**Light User (30 messages/month):**
- Mostly quick queries: "What's networking?" "Review this bullet point"
- Occasional deep dive: "Help me think through this career move"
- **Free tier (100 msgs):** More than enough
- **Would they upgrade?** Probably not (limits aren't the constraint)

**Moderate User (80 messages/month):**
- Mix of quick queries and coaching sessions
- 2-3 deep strategic conversations per month
- Regular resume/cover letter feedback
- **Free tier (100 msgs):** Adequate, might hit limits occasionally
- **Would they upgrade?** Maybe, if they value the community and want premium AI

**Power User (200+ messages/month):**
- Multiple deep coaching sessions (5-7 per month like /avengers-assemble)
- Frequent quick queries throughout the week
- Heavy engagement with Navigator
- **Free tier (100 msgs):** Hits limits regularly
- **Would they upgrade?** Yes (needs the capacity + values premium Sonnet)

### What Doesn't Count Toward Limits

**Unlimited (Free and Paid):**
- Human-to-human DMs
- Collab Space discussions
- Community conversations
- Thread comments/replies
- Real-time chat

**Only Navigator AI queries count toward limits.**

**Why This Matters:**
- Users can collaborate freely without anxiety about burning tokens
- AI is treated as premium infrastructure resource (fair to meter)
- Community connection is unlimited (aligns with collaboration principle)

## TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

### Dynamic Prompting + RAG (Not Custom LLMs)

**What We're Building:**
- Community-specific prompts stored in database
- Document upload + URL ingestion for knowledge base
- Vector database (RAG) for context retrieval
- Modular prompt composition at runtime

**What We're NOT Building:**
- Custom fine-tuned models per community
- Model hosting infrastructure
- Model deployment pipelines
- Complex MLOps

**Community Setup Flow:**

**Option A: Conversational (Initial Cartographer Session)**
1. Community builder has deep conversation with Cartographer
2. Extracts frameworks, principles, community knowledge
3. Generates custom prompt module automatically
4. Preview/test before going live

**Option B: Upload (For Technical Builders)**
1. Upload community guidelines, FAQs, methodology docs
2. Provide website URL for content scraping
3. Voyager processes and creates prompt + RAG knowledge base
4. Builder reviews and refines

**Both Options Result In:**
- Custom prompt module (stored in DB)
- RAG knowledge base (vector embeddings)
- Community-specific Navigator behavior

**Runtime Flow:**
```
User query â†’
  Load community prompt â†’
  RAG retrieval (relevant docs) â†’
  Complexity classification â†’
  Route to model (Haiku/Llama/Sonnet) â†’
  Compose final prompt â†’
  Generate response
```

**Scalability:**
- No per-community infrastructure costs
- Shared RAG database across communities
- Same API calls to Claude/Llama providers
- Marginal cost per community: ~$0

## WHY GROWTH-ALIGNED REVENUE SHARE?

### The Problem with Traditional Models

**Flat Revenue Share (like Patreon 90/10):**
- Builder gets same % whether 100 or 100K members
- No incentive to scale (effort vs reward doesn't scale)
- Platform can't afford generous terms at small scale

**Subscription Pricing (like Circle, Discourse):**
- Builder pays platform regardless of success
- All risk on builder (pay even if community fails)
- Zero revenue share (platform takes, doesn't give)

### Our Solution: Growth-Aligned Tiers

**The Philosophy:**
"We're betting on you. Start for free. As you grow, we grow together. At scale, you're our partner."

**How It Works:**
- **Early stage (0-1K):** You focus on building, we cover costs (5% is symbolic)
- **Growth stage (1K-50K):** You're gaining traction, we reward that (10-20%)
- **Scale stage (100K-1M):** You're successful, we share meaningfully (30%)
- **Mega scale (1M+):** You're a partner, we split 50/50

**Why This Is Better:**

**For Small Communities:**
- Pay nothing upfront (vs $50-500/month competitors charge)
- 5% of small revenue = we subsidize your growth
- Focus on community, not infrastructure bills

**For Growing Communities:**
- Increasing share = incentive to keep growing
- Your success = our success (aligned incentives)
- No surprise fee increases (transparent tiers)

**For Large Communities:**
- 30-50% = life-changing income ($270K+/month at 1M)
- Zero chance of leaving (why rebuild elsewhere?)
- True partnership (we both win big)

**For Voyager:**
- Better margins at small scale (when we need them)
- Strong retention at large scale (builder incentivized to stay)
- Differentiated positioning (no competitor does this)

### The Math That Makes It Work

**At 100K members:**
- Flat 30%: Builder gets $28K, Voyager gets $66K (61% margin)
- Growth tiers: Builder gets $14K, Voyager gets $81K (68% margin)
- **We gain $14K/month in profit** âœ…

**At 1M members:**
- Flat 30%: Builder gets $285K, Voyager gets $665K (66% margin)
- Growth tiers: Builder gets $271K, Voyager gets $679K (66% margin)
- **Margins similar, but builder is locked in (earning $3.2M/year!)** âœ…

**The Win:**
- Better economics for us at scale where we need it (small-medium communities)
- Better retention at mega scale (50% share = never leaving)
- Clear growth story for builders (visible path to massive income)

## RISKS & MITIGATIONS

### Risk 1: Unit Economics Don't Work

**Risk:** Costs too high, revenue share too generous, margins negative

**Mitigation:**
- Model costs at different scales (terminal with Marcus)
- Test with pilot (real data, not assumptions)
- Adjust revenue share % if needed (before scaling)
- Optimize compute costs (caching, efficiency)

---

### Risk 2: Conversion Rate Too Low

**Risk:** <1% of members upgrade, revenue insufficient

**Mitigation:**
- Make personalization genuinely valuable (not gimmick)
- Emphasize patronage model (supporting community)
- Show value (what % goes to builder)
- A/B test pricing ($5 vs $10 vs $15)

---

### Risk 3: Migration Friction Too High

**Risk:** Community builder says yes, but members don't migrate

**Mitigation:**
- Phased rollout (10% then 100%)
- Incentivize early adopters (founder perks)
- Clear communication (why we're moving)
- Make onboarding frictionless (2-minute signup)

---

### Risk 4: Custom LLM Doesn't Deliver

**Risk:** Navigator answers <90% of questions, value prop fails

**Mitigation:**
- Quality control (test before launch)
- Cartographer feedback loop (continuous improvement)
- Fallback to human support (when AI can't answer)
- Manage expectations (90% is goal, not guarantee)

---

### Risk 5: Support Overwhelm

**Risk:** 100K users = too many support requests

**Mitigation:**
- AI-first support (Navigator answers support questions)
- Community builder helps (they know their community)
- Tiered support (free AI, paid human)
- Scale support team with revenue

---

### Risk 6: Community Builder Dependency

**Risk:** Too much revenue from one community, they leave = crisis

**Mitigation:**
- Diversify (10+ communities, not just 1-2)
- High switching cost (custom LLM, revenue stream)
- Contract terms (annual commitment)
- Build ecosystem (network effects = stickiness)
